Overview

Anatomy of the Thyroid

The thyroid is a gland that has the shape of a butterfly and is located at the front and base of the neck. It is a vital hormone gland that plays a major role in our metabolism, regulation of body temperature, heart rate, blood pressure, and growth and development of the human body. It does all of this by releasing a steady amount of thyroid hormones into the bloodstream.

What are thyroid diseases?

Thyroid diseases are basically any dysfunction occurring in the thyroid gland. Thyroid diseases are common problems that can cause many symptoms like nervousness, fast heart rate, unintentional weight loss, and more. The US has an estimate of about 20 million that have some form of thyroid disease and about 60% of those with thyroid disease are unaware of their condition. In addition, women are also more likely to have any dysfunction in the thyroid gland.The bad news is that the causes of thyroid problems are largely unknown.
